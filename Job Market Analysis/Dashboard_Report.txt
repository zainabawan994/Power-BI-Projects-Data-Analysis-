Job Market Analysis Dashboard Report
1. Introduction

This dashboard report presents an analysis of job postings data including job titles, experience requirements, company information, applicants, salary ranges, and job locations. The purpose of this dashboard is to understand job market trends, hiring patterns, and demand for different roles and experience levels.

The dashboard was created to help analyze recruitment trends, job demand by industry, experience requirements, and applicant behavior.

2. Dataset Description

The dataset used in this dashboard contains the following columns:

Job Posting ID
Job Posting Date
Job Title
Job Title Full
Job Title Additional Info
Job Position Type
Job Position Level
Years of Experience
Job Skills
Job Location
Minimum Pay
Maximum Pay
Pay Rate
Number of Applicants
Company Name
Company Industry
Company Size

These attributes help analyze job demand, salary trends, company hiring patterns, and applicant distribution.

3. Dashboard Overview

The dashboard includes the following visualizations:

Job postings by year
Applicants by year
Experience level distribution
Job roles distribution
Company size vs applicants
Industry-wise job postings
Job location map
Experience vs job roles
Filters for Month, Industry, and Job Level

These visuals help users interact with the data and explore hiring trends.

4. Key Insights from Dashboard
4.1 Job Posting Trends

The number of job postings increased from 2018 to 2021.
This indicates that the job market demand is growing over time, especially in recent years.

4.2 Applicants Trend

The total number of applicants recorded is 209.
The number of applicants increased in recent years, which shows increasing competition in the job market.

4.3 Experience Requirements

The average years of experience required is 2.67 years.
Most jobs are for:

Junior level
Mid-level

This means the dataset mainly represents early-career and mid-career opportunities.

4.4 Job Roles Demand

The most common job roles observed are:

Software Engineer
Data Analyst
Data Engineer

This shows that technical and data-related jobs are in high demand.

4.5 Company Size Analysis

Large companies receive more job applications compared to small companies.
This suggests that job seekers prefer applying to larger organizations.

4.6 Industry Analysis

The Education Management industry shows significant hiring activity compared to some other industries in the dataset.

4.7 Job Type Analysis

Most job postings are Full-Time positions, while part-time and contract jobs are fewer.

4.8 Location Analysis

Most jobs are located in the United States, indicating that the dataset is mainly focused on the US job market.

4.9 Salary Data Issue

Minimum Pay and Maximum Pay values are missing or blank in the dashboard, which means salary analysis cannot be fully performed. This is a limitation of the dataset.

5. Dashboard Filters

The dashboard includes interactive filters for:

Month
Industry
Job Position Level (Junior / Mid Level)

These filters allow users to analyze job postings for specific time periods, industries, and experience levels.

6. Conclusion

The dashboard analysis shows that job postings and applicant numbers are increasing over time, indicating a growing and competitive job market. Most job opportunities are available for junior and mid-level candidates, especially in technical roles such as Software Engineering and Data Analysis. Larger companies attract more applicants, and most jobs are full-time positions located in the United States.

Overall, this dashboard helps understand hiring trends, job demand, and experience requirements in the job market.
